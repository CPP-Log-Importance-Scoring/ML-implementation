# Sim-Real Train→Test Report — Incident Formation & Accuracy
_Generated 2026-06-22 · branch `dev` (fixes #1 severity-gate + #3 parser-severity merged)_

## Setup
Production-like corpus (`data/raw/sim_real/`, generated by `scripts/gen_sim_real_dataset.py`):

| Split | Days | Composition |
|---|---|---|
| **train** | 16 | 14 clean + 2 anomaly (CPU_OVERLOAD, LINK_FLAPPING) — ~12% incident rate |
| **test_clean** | 3 | held-out clean days |
| **test_anomaly** | 3 | held-out, **novel** scenarios absent from training: PROTOCOL_STARVATION, OOM_KILL_CASCADE, SPLIT_BRAIN + MTU_MISMATCH |

Model cold-started, trained on the corpus, then **frozen** (retrain trigger disabled) so every test day is scored by the same model — a proper held-out evaluation.

> Note: although 34,064 training rows were available, the persisted model trained on only **~1,033 rows** — the rolling store keeps the last 50 sessions (≈⅓ of one day). The effective training baseline is the tail of the clean training days.

Incident size across all 6 days: n=47, median **18** rows, **max 861** rows, 10 incidents > 100 rows.

**Two structural defects:**
1. **Over-merging.** Incidents span up to **22.9 hours** / 861 logs — the temporal clusterer glues whole-day, unrelated activity into a single "incident." An incident that covers 00:00–18:06 is not an incident.
2. **Critical rows are excluded from incidents.** Across every day, **0 of the critical-labelled rows land inside any incident** (21 critical OOM rows on 04-21 are all unclustered). Incidents are built from bulk medium/low noise, not the severe lines — so root-cause selection picks benign high-frequency logs (`"Protocol frame received"`, `"ACL policy enforced"`, `"Redundancy init complete - NORMAL"`), never the actual cause.

## Accuracy

**Detection (recall at "medium or above") — strong, and it generalises:**

| Ground-truth window | Severity | Result | Tier assigned |
|---|---|---|---|
| PROTOCOL_STARVATION 14:30–14:30 | CRITICAL | ✅ DETECTED | medium |
| OOM_KILL_CASCADE 16:43–17:00 | CRITICAL | ✅ DETECTED | medium |
| SPLIT_BRAIN 03:00–03:05 | CRITICAL | ✅ DETECTED | medium |
| MTU_MISMATCH 11:00–11:45 | MEDIUM_HIGH | ✅ DETECTED | medium |

**4/4 novel anomaly windows flagged** — the model raises every injected incident to at least `medium` even though these scenario types were absent from training. Recall is real.

**Severity accuracy — systematically one tier too low:** 0/4 reached `critical`. Every CRITICAL anomaly was under-labelled `medium`. The anomaly-window rows are INFO-severity bursts, so `final_score` is dominated by the ML term, which the `SCORING_ML_WEIGHT=0.5` ceiling caps below the 0.65 critical cutoff.

**Precision (clean days) — poor at the incident level:** all 3 clean days produced **only `medium` incidents** (3, 8, 7). A "medium incident" appears on *every* day, clean or not — so it carries no discriminative power. You cannot tell a clean day from an anomaly day by its incidents. (Row-level critical false positives were low: 4 / 0 / 0 across the clean days — fixes #1/#3 hold there.)

## Verdict
- ✅ **Detection works and generalises** — novel anomalies are reliably surfaced (100% window recall at medium+).
- ❌ **Severity is systematically under-tiered** — CRITICAL → medium (the ML weight ceiling, confirmed earlier and reproduced here on novel data).
- ❌ **Incident formation is the weakest link** — day-spanning over-merged blobs; the severe rows are excluded from incidents; root causes are benign noise.
- ❌ **No incident-level precision** — clean and anomaly days look identical (a few medium incidents each).

## Highest-leverage next steps (consistent with prior findings)
1. **Fix incident clustering** before anything else: tighten the temporal/correlation grouping so incidents are minutes, not hours, and **include the high-severity rows** as cluster seeds. Today's blobs make root cause meaningless.
2. **Train on a frozen clean baseline** with a real window (not 50 sessions) so `combined_score` is stable and separable — the precondition for any severity-tier fix.
3. Only then revisit the **critical-tier threshold/weight** — with stable scores and tight incidents, severity ranking becomes tractable without overfitting.
